The History of Loud

"Loud" started out as a little drawing in the corner of a notepad on one of those peaceful days when your thoughts are free to roam. Slowly, what began as a single sketch developed into a narrative about emotion, identity, and finding strength in the midst of turmoil. Loud served as both my creative release and a means of using comics to convey a more profound, poignant tale. It was inspired by city life, anime, and my own experiences. After several coffee runs, late nights, and digital rewrites, I can now honestly say that I'm proud of it.

What is Loud About?

Following the protagonist, Loud. He is a teenage afro latino kid that moved into the city with his mom now attending highschool. He has to get used to the weird changes in his life, changes that a normal kid cound't fathem.

After a concert, Loud and his best friend Wayne get cornered in an alley while heading to the subway. The mugging triggers something inside Loud—an ability he never knew he had, one tied deeply to his emotions and mental state. His powers are unpredictable, raw, and constantly evolving. Now, he must navigate not just the chaos of high school, but a power that grows louder with every feeling he suppresses, everchanging along with his mood.

It’s a story about emotion, growth, friendship, and learning to live with the noise inside your head.
